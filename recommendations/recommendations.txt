1. Double down on Electronics as a growth engine.
Increase investment in high-performing products within this category through marketing, availability, and expansion of product lines.
Focus on both scale and margin optimization.
2. Conduct margin optimization across top-selling products.
Identify products with high sales but lower profitability and evaluate:
Pricing strategy
Cost structure
Discounting patterns
Goal: Improve profit without sacrificing volume.
3. Reevaluate the Office category strategy.
Perform a deep dive to determine whether to:
Optimize (pricing / assortment / positioning)
Or reduce investment
This category currently underperforms and may impact overall efficiency.
4. Leverage West region as a performance benchmark.
Analyze:
Product mix
Customer behavior
Sales strategies
Apply learnings to underperforming regions to drive consistency.
5. Introduce seasonality-driven planning.
Align:
Marketing campaigns
Inventory management
Sales initiatives
with high-performing months.
Goal: maximize peak periods and stabilize low-performing ones.
6. Reduce dependency on a small set of products.
Diversify revenue streams by:
Promoting mid-performing products
Expanding successful categories
This reduces business risk and improves stability.
7. Shift focus from revenue to profit-driven decision-making.
Establish profit-based KPIs such as:
Profit Margin by Product
Profit Contribution by Category
Profit per Order
This ensures sustainable growth, not just top-line growth.
